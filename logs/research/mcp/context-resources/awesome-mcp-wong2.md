## Scope
Structured into three tiers: **Reference Servers** (7 entries demonstrating MCP features and SDKs), **Official Servers** (~200 entries, maintained by companies), **Community Servers** (~300 entries). Total ~500+ servers. Alphabetical within each tier. Submissions happen through a dedicated website rather than GitHub PRs.

## Takeaway summary
Tiered structure — reference / official / community — is the clearest signal in the awesome-list space for "is this a toy or is someone responsible for it?" When evaluating a candidate server, the tier tells you how much to trust its maintenance claims. Website-based submission is unusual for awesome-lists and means the list isn't strictly community-editable via PR; it may lag more edge-case submissions but curation is more consistent.

## Use for
- Which MCP servers are "official" (company-maintained) vs community experiments?
- A tiered view when punkpeye's flat list is overwhelming

## Relationship to other resources
Peer of `awesome-mcp-punkpeye.md` and `awesome-mcp-appcypher.md`. Tiered taxonomy is the distinguishing feature; useful when punkpeye's is too flat.

## Quality notes
Website-submission gate means the list curator has final say; quality tends to be more consistent than PR-based lists, at the cost of coverage breadth.
